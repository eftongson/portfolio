Levi's Tailor Shop

Out of Home

Several Levi's branches in the US have the Tailor Shop, a lesser-known feature that provides custom
embroidery, distressing, and of course, tailoring. How can Levi's show customers that this feature exists?

These were created during the One Club for Creativity's four-day October 2018 San Francisco Creative Bootcamp.
Art Direction: Emilia Tongson, Fangqi Li
Copywriting: Stephon Allen, Ian Blackley, Amanda Wennberg


Goal: Promote Levi's Tailor Shop, a feature of Levi's retailers where customers can destroy or build upon their Levi's.

Idea: Appeal to customers by focusing on the personal aspect of the service, comparing it to the act of getting a meaningful tattoo.
Levi's has a long history of being associated with youth, a bit of risk, and being rough around the edges, yet still maintaining timelessness. In today's culture, tattoos still carry the risk of some pain, but have become less taboo, and more a symbol of creativity.

Execution: Build curiosity by commissioning street art that suggest the idea while only hinting the brand. Then, create a series of out-of-home ads in the areas near the Tailor Shop locations that show the personalized denim on an inked wearer, with taglines that apply to both skin and denim.
