A closed mouth does not get fed.

This statement, as said by orientation speaker and 4A's associate Isiah Thompson, was one of the biggest takeaways I've had since my second MAIP experience.

Speaking up has always been a challenge for me, especially as an immigrant. In line with Filipinx culture, I was raised to stay quiet, to be obedient, and to remain humble. However, I've learned that in order to be a successful designer and art director, I need to not only be clear in getting my visual ideas across, but also be able to tell a story and actively communicate with those around me.

Between my first MAIP experience and now, I've realized that my job isn't only to produce work, but to be in an environment where I must be unafraid to share and discuss with others in order to create the best work, and to foster an open, collaborative environment.

In hearing Isiah speak about speaking up, I was reminded that I am worthy of having a seat at the table, and should be more comfortable in taking up space, especially when voicing my needs and sharing my talents with the world.
